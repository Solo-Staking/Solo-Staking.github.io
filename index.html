What is Solo Staking?

Solo Staking is the Ethereum path for running your own validator instead of delegating to a pool or service. The official Staking Launchpad guides client choice, validator key generation, and deposits to the Ethereum staking deposit contract.

How Solo Staking works

  1. Check readinessReview hardware, uptime, key security, and maintenance responsibilities before risking funds.
  2. Choose clientsSelect execution and consensus clients, with attention to client diversity and update practices.
  3. Generate keysCreate validator keys securely and keep withdrawal credentials under your control.
  4. Deposit and monitorUse the official launchpad, verify the domain, deposit ETH, then keep the node online and maintained.

You keep control of validator and withdrawal keys; key handling and backups are core responsibilities.

Operational discipline

Uptime, client updates, and slashing prevention matter; review ethereum.org guidance before depositing.

Solo Staking FAQ

What is solo staking?

Solo staking means running an Ethereum node and validator yourself, depositing ETH to activate the validator, and taking responsibility for uptime, keys, and maintenance.

Is solo staking the same as pooled staking?

No. Solo staking is self-operated and validator-based, while pooled staking lets users contribute smaller amounts through a pool or liquid staking protocol.

How much ETH is needed for solo staking?

A validator requires 32 ETH to activate. Modern validator balances can hold more, but users should confirm current rules in the official launchpad and docs.

Can solo staking be done from home?

Yes. Ethereum documentation describes home staking as running your own node with internet-connected hardware and validator software.

Where should I start solo staking?

Start from the official Ethereum Staking Launchpad and verify the domain before connecting a wallet or depositing ETH.
